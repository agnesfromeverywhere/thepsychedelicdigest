## How do I start microdosing?

Start by choosing a protocol (the Fadiman Protocol is the most common starting point), sourcing a precise scale that measures to 0.01g, and beginning with a low dose. For dried psilocybin mushrooms, 0.1g is a sensible starting point. Take your first dose on a low-pressure day with no important obligations. Record your experience in a journal from day one. The goal on your first dose is observation, not transformation. For a full guide, see the article on [how to start microdosing](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog) on this site.

## What is the best microdosing protocol?

The Fadiman Protocol is the most widely used and best documented. It follows a one day on, two days off cycle repeated over four to eight weeks. It was developed by psychedelic researcher Dr James Fadiman and has the largest body of self-reported data behind it. Alternatives include the Stamets Stack (four days on, three days off, with lion's mane and niacin) and the Every Other Day protocol. For most beginners, the Fadiman Protocol is the best starting point.

## Is microdosing legal in Australia?

For most Australians, no. Possessing psilocybin mushrooms for personal use remains illegal in all states except the ACT, where small amounts were decriminalised for personal possession in 2023. In 2023, the TGA rescheduled psilocybin to allow authorised psychiatrists to prescribe it for treatment-resistant depression in clinical settings. This is a therapeutic access pathway, not a personal use framework. Microdosing for personal wellness purposes remains outside the law in most of Australia. For a full breakdown, see the article on [microdosing law in Australia](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog) on this site.

## What are the benefits of microdosing psilocybin?

The most consistently reported benefits in observational research and community observation are improved mood, reduced social anxiety, greater emotional openness, enhanced creative thinking, and improved focus on complex cognitive tasks. Research from Vince Polito, James Fadiman, and Imperial College London supports these reports, though controlled trial evidence remains limited. Microdosing is not a treatment for any specific clinical condition, and benefits vary significantly between individuals.

## How much should I microdose?

For dried psilocybin mushrooms, most practitioners start at 0.1g and calibrate to between 0.1g and 0.3g. For LSD, the typical microdose range is 5 to 15 micrograms. A correctly calibrated microdose produces no hallucinations, no perceptual impairment, and ideally no immediately noticeable effect. If you feel any significant alteration in perception, your dose is too high. Reduce it and try again on your next scheduled dose day. ## What is the Fadiman protocol?

The Fadiman Protocol is a microdosing schedule consisting of one dose day followed by two consecutive non-dose days, repeated in a continuous cycle over four to eight weeks. Developed by Dr James Fadiman, it is designed to prevent tolerance buildup and allow the nervous system adequate recovery between doses. Many practitioners report that day two of the cycle, the first non-dose day after a dose, carries a positive afterglow effect and is often their most productive day. A full explanation is available in the dedicated Fadiman Protocol article on [thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog).

## Can you microdose and work?

Yes, for most people at a correctly calibrated dose. The key word is calibrated. A true microdose, sub-perceptual and below any impairment threshold, should be compatible with ordinary work, including focused cognitive tasks and social interactions. Many knowledge workers, creatives, and healthcare practitioners in the community report microdosing during their working day with positive results for focus, creativity, and emotional presence. A dose that is too high, however, will produce restlessness and impair rather than enhance performance. Start low, know your dose, and if in any doubt, begin your protocol on a non-working day. For more on this, see the article on [microdosing and productivity](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog).

## What mushrooms are used for microdosing?

The most common psilocybin-containing mushroom used for microdosing is Psilocybe cubensis. It is the most widely cultivated, most consistently available, and most studied. Other psilocybin species include Psilocybe semilanceata (liberty caps), which are more potent gram-for-gram. Beyond psilocybin mushrooms, lion's mane (Hericium erinaceus) is a non-psychedelic functional mushroom used alongside microdosing protocols for its neurogenesis-supporting properties. It is the key ingredient in the Stamets Stack protocol.
