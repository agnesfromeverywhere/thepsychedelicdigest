These are the eight questions I am asked most often about microdosing, across six years of running the @psychedelictherapyclub community and publishing at The Psychedelic Digest. I have answered each one as directly and honestly as I can.

## How do I start microdosing?

Start with a protocol, a scale, and a journal. The [Fadiman Protocol](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/the-fadiman-protocol-explained-2026) is the most widely used and well-documented starting point: one day on, two days off, repeated over four to eight weeks. For dried psilocybin mushrooms, begin at 0.1g. A precision scale measuring to 0.01g is non-negotiable because potency varies significantly between batches. Take your first dose on a low-pressure day with no important obligations. Record everything you notice. The goal on day one is observation, not transformation.

For the full beginner guide, see [How to Start Microdosing](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/how-to-start-microdosing-2026) on this site.

## What is the best microdosing protocol?

For most beginners, the Fadiman Protocol is the best starting point. It is the most studied, the most documented, and the most consistently reported to produce positive outcomes across community observation. Alternatives include the Stamets Stack (four days on, three days off, with lion's mane and niacin) and the Every Other Day protocol. The Stamets Stack may suit people specifically focused on neurogenesis. The Every Other Day protocol is simpler but carries a higher tolerance accumulation risk over time.

Research from [Vince Polito at Macquarie University](https://researchers.mq.edu.au/en/persons/vince-polito), who has run some of the most rigorous microdosing studies to date, consistently identifies structured protocols with rest days as producing the most reliable outcomes.

## Is microdosing legal in Australia?

For most Australians, no. Possession of psilocybin mushrooms for personal use remains illegal in all states and territories except the ACT, where small amounts were decriminalised for personal possession in 2023. In July 2023, the [TGA](https://www.tga.gov.au) rescheduled psilocybin to allow authorised psychiatrists to prescribe it for treatment-resistant depression in clinical settings. This is a therapeutic access pathway with strict eligibility criteria, not a personal use framework. Microdosing for personal wellness remains outside the law in most of Australia. ## What are the benefits of microdosing psilocybin?

The most consistently reported benefits in both observational research and community observation are improved mood, reduced social anxiety, greater emotional openness, enhanced creative thinking, and improved capacity to focus on complex work. A 2021 study published in [eLife](https://elifesciences.org) analysing self-reports from 953 microdosers found improvements across mood, focus, and psychological wellbeing. These findings are supported by observational data from James Fadiman's ongoing research project. The research is promising but not yet conclusive, and benefits vary significantly between individuals. Microdosing is not a treatment for any specific clinical condition.

## How much should I microdose?

For dried psilocybin mushrooms, start at 0.1g and calibrate to between 0.1g and 0.3g based on your individual response. For LSD, the standard starting range is 5 to 15 micrograms. A correctly calibrated microdose should produce no hallucinations, no perceptual impairment, and ideally no immediately noticeable effect. If you feel clearly altered within the first hour, your dose is too high. Step down and try again on your next scheduled dose day. ## What is the Fadiman protocol?

The Fadiman Protocol is a microdosing schedule consisting of one dose day followed by two consecutive non-dose days, repeated continuously over four to eight weeks. Developed by psychedelic researcher Dr James Fadiman based on data from thousands of self-reported participant observations, it is designed to prevent tolerance buildup and give the nervous system adequate recovery between doses. Many practitioners report that day two of the cycle carries a subtle positive afterglow and is their most focused and productive day. A full explanation of how and why it works is available in the dedicated [Fadiman Protocol guide](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/the-fadiman-protocol-explained-2026) on this site.

## Can you microdose and work?

Yes, for most people at a correctly calibrated dose. A true microdose is sub-perceptual and should be fully compatible with ordinary work, focused cognitive tasks, and normal social interaction. Many knowledge workers, creatives, and healthcare practitioners in the community report microdosing during working hours with meaningful improvements in focus, creative fluency, and emotional presence with colleagues and clients. A dose that is even slightly too high, however, will produce restlessness and impair performance. Start low, know your dose precisely, and if in any doubt, begin your protocol on a non-working day. ## What mushrooms are used for microdosing?

The most common psilocybin-containing mushroom used for microdosing is Psilocybe cubensis, which is the most widely cultivated and most consistently available. Other psilocybin species include Psilocybe semilanceata (liberty caps), which are more potent gram-for-gram. Beyond psilocybin mushrooms, lion's mane (Hericium erinaceus) is a non-psychedelic functional mushroom widely used alongside microdosing for its nerve growth factor-stimulating properties. It is the key non-psychedelic ingredient in the Stamets Stack protocol.
