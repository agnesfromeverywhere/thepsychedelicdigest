**TL;DR:** The Stamets Stack combines low-dose psilocybin with lion's mane mushroom and niacin, with the goal of enhancing neuroplasticity beyond what psilocybin alone produces. The individual components have solid evidence. The specific combination is less studied. Here is what we know.

## What Is the Stamets Stack?

The Stamets Stack is a microdosing protocol that combines three substances:

- **Psilocybin mushrooms** at a sub-perceptual dose, typically 0.1 to 0.3 grams of dried material
- **Lion's mane mushroom** (Hericium erinaceus), typically 500mg to 1000mg
- **Niacin** (Vitamin B3), typically 100mg to 200mg

The protocol involves taking this combination for five consecutive days, followed by two days off. This differs from the Fadiman Protocol, which uses one day on and two days off.

The stack was developed and popularised by mycologist Paul Stamets, who presented it at a psychedelic science conference and has since discussed it widely in public forums including the Joe Rogan Experience podcast.

---

## Who Is Paul Stamets?

Paul Stamets is one of the world's most recognised mycologists. He has spent decades researching the medicinal and ecological properties of fungi, holds multiple patents related to mushroom-derived compounds, and is the founder of Fungi Perfecti, a company that produces functional mushroom supplements.

He is also a scientific advisor to the Beckley Foundation and has collaborated with researchers at several major institutions studying psilocybin.

His credibility in the mushroom science world is substantial. His claims about the specific combination in his stack are more speculative, which is exactly what makes it worth examining carefully.

## What Does Each Component Do?

### Psilocybin

Psilocybin binds to serotonin 5-HT2A receptors and produces measurable changes in brain connectivity even at sub-perceptual doses. The core documented effects at microdose levels include increased neuroplasticity, reduced default mode network activity, and improvements in mood and emotional regulation.

For a full breakdown of what psilocybin does at microdose levels, read our article on [what microdosing actually does to your brain](https://www.thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/microdosing-benefits-2026).

### Lion's Mane Mushroom

Lion's mane has the strongest evidence base of any functional mushroom for neurological benefit. It stimulates the production of **Nerve Growth Factor (NGF)** and **Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF)**, proteins that support the growth, maintenance, and repair of nerve cells.

A 2019 study published in Biomedical Research found that lion's mane supplementation produced significant improvements in mild cognitive impairment over 16 weeks. A 2023 study from the University of Queensland found that lion's mane compounds promoted new neural connections at speeds previously unseen in a controlled lab setting.

### Niacin (Vitamin B3)

Niacin is Stamets' most speculative addition. His theory is that niacin acts as a "flushing" agent that helps drive psilocybin and lion's mane compounds across the blood-brain barrier and into peripheral nervous system tissue, potentially amplifying neuroplastic effects.

The evidence for this specific mechanism is not yet published in peer-reviewed literature. Niacin does cause peripheral vasodilation, which is what produces the characteristic skin flush. Whether this meaningfully enhances the delivery of the other compounds is unclear.

At doses above 200mg, niacin can cause uncomfortable flushing, redness, and tingling. Start low.

## What Is the Theory Behind Combining Them?

Stamets' hypothesis is that psilocybin opens a window of neuroplasticity and lion's mane provides the raw material for new neural growth during that window. Niacin facilitates delivery of both compounds to peripheral nerve tissue.

The metaphor he uses is that psilocybin tills the soil, lion's mane plants the seeds, and niacin waters them.

It is a compelling model. Whether it holds up to rigorous clinical scrutiny is still being tested.

## What Does the Research Show?

As of 2026, there are no published randomised controlled trials specifically testing the Stamets Stack as a combined protocol. Research exists for each component separately, but not for the specific three-way combination at the doses Stamets recommends.

What does exist:

- Strong evidence for lion's mane's neuroplasticity benefits individually
- Moderate evidence for psilocybin's neuroplasticity effects at microdose levels
- Theoretical but unproven evidence for niacin's role in the stack
- Multiple anecdotal reports, including from researchers, suggesting the combination produces stronger neuroplastic effects than psilocybin alone

A clinical trial specifically testing the Stamets Stack is reportedly in development but has not yet published results.

## How to Try the Stamets Stack

If you want to try the stack, here is what a responsible approach looks like:

1. **Start with Fadiman first.** Run at least four weeks of a standard Fadiman protocol before adding lion's mane and niacin. This gives you a baseline.

2. **Add lion's mane first.** Run the 5-days-on-2-days-off schedule with psilocybin and lion's mane only before introducing niacin.

3. **Introduce niacin at a low dose.** Start at 50mg and work up. Flushing at this level is mild. At 200mg it can be uncomfortable for sensitive individuals.

4. **Keep tracking.** The only way to know if the stack is working better than psilocybin alone is to have a clear baseline from your earlier Fadiman period.
