# Psychedelic Therapy in Australia: Who Qualifies, What It Costs, and How to Access It in 2026

Psychedelic therapy is legal in Australia. Since 1 July 2023, authorised psychiatrists can prescribe psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression and MDMA for PTSD under strict clinical controls. Australia was the first country in the world to establish this national regulated pathway.

Most people have heard the headline. Almost nobody knows what to actually do with that information.

If you have treatment-resistant depression or PTSD and you are wondering whether this applies to you, the answer is: possibly yes. But the path from curiosity to a clinical session is not obvious. The information is scattered across government websites, buried in academic papers, or wrapped in jargon that assumes you already know what an "Authorised Prescriber Scheme" is.

This guide fixes that. No jargon. No hype. Just what you need to know.

---

## What Is Psychedelic Therapy?

Psychedelic therapy is a structured clinical treatment that combines a psychedelic substance with professional psychological support. It is not the same as taking a substance and hoping for the best.

Researchers and clinicians describe three essential phases:

**Preparation:** A series of talk-therapy sessions with a trained therapist before any medicine is taken. The goal is to build therapeutic trust, explore the patient's history, and set a clear intention for the experience ahead.

**The dosing session:** The patient takes the prescribed medicine in a clinical setting, lying down, typically with an eye mask and music. One or two trained therapists are present throughout. Each session lasts six or more hours.

**Integration:** A series of therapy sessions after the dosing experience to make sense of what surfaced, translate insights into lasting change, and process anything difficult or confusing. This phase can continue for weeks or months.

Without all three phases, the treatment is incomplete. The medicine is the key. The therapy is the door.

## Is Psychedelic Therapy Legal in Australia?

Yes. As of 1 July 2023, psychedelic therapy is legal in Australia for two specific conditions.

The TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration) rescheduled psilocybin and MDMA from Schedule 9 (prohibited substances) to Schedule 8 (controlled medicines) for specific clinical uses. Outside those approved uses, both substances remain prohibited.

Australia is the first country in the world to establish a national, regulated clinical model for these therapies.

---

## Who Qualifies for Psychedelic Therapy in Australia?

Currently, two conditions are approved:

**Psilocybin** is approved for **treatment-resistant depression (TRD)**, defined as depression that has not responded to at least two conventional treatments such as antidepressants or psychotherapy.

**MDMA** is approved for **post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)**.

Some people with co-occurring mental health conditions may also be eligible. However, people with a personal or family history of bipolar disorder, psychosis, or certain other psychiatric conditions are not considered suitable under current safety protocols.

If you do not qualify today, the framework is designed to evolve as research expands. Clinical trials into psilocybin for anxiety, addiction, and OCD are active in Australia right now.

## How Do You Access Psychedelic Therapy in Australia? Step by Step

### Step 1: Start with your GP

Your general practitioner cannot prescribe psilocybin or MDMA, but they are the first step. Explain your diagnosis, your treatment history, and what has not worked. Ask for a referral to a psychiatrist with experience in psychedelic-assisted therapy. The referral is your entry point into the system.

### Step 2: Find an authorised psychiatrist

Not every psychiatrist can prescribe these medicines. Only psychiatrists registered with AHPRA with a specialist registration in psychiatry and a completed Fellowship with the RANZCP are permitted to apply for Authorised Prescriber status.

The clearest public resource for finding one is the RANZCP "Find a Psychiatrist" directory at ranzcp.org, where authorised prescribers can nominate themselves. Mind Medicine Australia also maintains a prescriber resource at mindmedicineaustralia.org.au.

### Step 3: Clinical assessment

Once connected with an authorised psychiatrist, you will undergo a thorough assessment to determine whether you are a suitable candidate. This is a real clinical gate, not a formality. People with certain psychiatric histories will not proceed past this point, and that is by design.

### Step 4: Preparation sessions

If approved, you will complete a series of talk-therapy sessions before any medicine is involved. These build the therapeutic relationship, clarify your intentions, and prepare you for the experience. The quality of this phase significantly influences what happens in the dosing session.

### Step 5: The dosing session

You take the prescribed medicine in a clinical setting. You lie down, typically with an eye mask on and music playing. One or two therapists sit with you throughout. You are not left alone at any point. A standard treatment model involves one to three dosing sessions, depending on the protocol and the condition being treated.

### Step 6: Integration

The dosing session is not the end of treatment. Integration therapy begins immediately after and continues across multiple sessions. This is where the clinical value is consolidated. Without it, insights fade or become destabilising.

## How Much Does Psychedelic Therapy Cost in Australia?

Psychedelic therapy is expensive. The main cost is psychiatrist and therapist time: a trained clinician must be present for every dosing session, which lasts six or more hours.

Average prices in 2025 sit at:

- **$10,000 AUD** for a single dosing session including preparation and integration
- **$30,000 AUD** for a full three-dose MDMA-assisted therapy program

That is beyond the reach of most Australians. The good news is the funding landscape shifted significantly in 2025.

**Medibank** (Australia's largest private health insurer) committed $10 million AUD to fund MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD for eligible members, in partnership with Emyria. This is a world first in psychedelic insurance coverage. Psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression has since been added to the same program.

**The Department of Veterans' Affairs** committed to funding psychedelic-assisted therapy for eligible veterans with PTSD, as part of a $740 million veterans rehabilitation plan.

If you have private health insurance, contact your insurer directly to ask whether coverage applies under your current policy or whether an upgrade could make you eligible.

## What About Microdosing? Is That Legal in Australia?

No. Microdosing is not covered by the 2023 TGA decision.

Microdosing involves taking sub-perceptual doses of psilocybin or LSD for mood, focus, or general wellbeing. Outside the clinical prescribing pathway, psilocybin remains a Schedule 9 prohibited substance in Australia. That means microdosing is not legal under current Australian law, regardless of the intention behind it.

## Is Psychedelic Therapy Safe?

Within a clinical setting, with proper preparation and integration, psychedelic therapy carries a meaningfully different risk profile from recreational use.

A global survey found that 86% of people who participated in supervised psychedelic sessions reported the experience as helpful. Just 1% said things got worse. By contrast, 4.2% of those who used psychedelics for unsupervised self-treatment reported visits to the emergency department.

The clinical container is not optional. This is not a medicine you take at home. It is a medicine you take inside a carefully constructed therapeutic relationship.

The TGA has already issued more than a dozen warning letters to clinics and media outlets for the unlawful promotion of MDMA and psilocybin. Be cautious of any provider that feels more like a retreat booking service than a clinical referral. The regulation exists for good reason.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is psilocybin legal in Australia?**
Psilocybin is legal only when prescribed by a TGA-authorised psychiatrist for treatment-resistant depression. For all other uses, including recreational and microdosing, it remains a Schedule 9 prohibited substance.

**Can my GP prescribe psychedelic therapy?**
No. Only psychiatrists who have completed a Fellowship with the RANZCP and received Authorised Prescriber status from the TGA can prescribe psilocybin or MDMA for therapy.

**How long does psychedelic therapy take?**
A full course of treatment typically spans several weeks to months. It includes multiple preparation sessions, one to three dosing sessions of six or more hours each, and an ongoing integration phase that continues after the dosing is complete.

**Does Medicare cover psychedelic therapy in Australia?**
Not currently. Medicare does not subsidise psychedelic-assisted therapy. Private insurers including Medibank have begun covering it for eligible members. Veterans may be eligible through the Department of Veterans' Affairs.

**What is the difference between MDMA therapy and psilocybin therapy?**
MDMA-assisted therapy is approved for PTSD. Psilocybin-assisted therapy is approved for treatment-resistant depression. They work differently: MDMA supports emotional openness and memory processing, while psilocybin induces a more immersive altered state often described as a journey. Both require a trained therapist present throughout.

**What if I do not qualify for psychedelic therapy right now?**
You can ask your psychiatrist about clinical trials. There are over 15 active psychedelic trials in Australia. Joining a trial provides supervised access to these treatments as part of ongoing research, often at lower or no cost.
