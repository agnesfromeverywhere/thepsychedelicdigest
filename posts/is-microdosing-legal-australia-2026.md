Is microdosing legal in Australia in 2026? For most Australians, no. Possessing or using psilocybin mushrooms for personal use remains a criminal offence in every state and territory except the ACT. That said, the legal landscape has shifted significantly since 2023 and continues to evolve. If you are trying to understand where things actually stand, here is a clear and current overview.

## What the TGA Changed in 2023

In February 2023, the Therapeutic Goods Administration made a landmark decision to reschedule psilocybin and MDMA. From 1 July 2023, psilocybin moved from Schedule 9 (Prohibited Substance) to Schedule 8 (Controlled Drug) for use in specific clinical settings. You can read the full decision on the [TGA website](https://www.tga.gov.au).

This made Australia one of the first countries in the world to allow these substances within a regulated medical framework. In practice, it means authorised psychiatrists can now legally prescribe psilocybin to patients with treatment-resistant depression under the Special Access Scheme. That is a clinical, supervised context requiring formal psychiatric diagnosis and an approved prescriber. It is not a personal use framework.

## What It Does Not Mean

The 2023 rescheduling does not legalise microdosing for personal wellness. Possessing psilocybin mushrooms without a prescription remains illegal. Growing them remains illegal. Purchasing them outside an authorised channel remains illegal.

For most Australians who are currently microdosing, the activity sits in a legally grey-to-illegal zone depending on the state. State laws govern possession offences and they vary considerably. The ACT decriminalised small amounts of personal drugs in 2023, meaning possession of small quantities of psilocybin there carries a civil infringement rather than a criminal charge. No other Australian state or territory has followed.

## State by State

In New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland, Western Australia, South Australia, Tasmania, and the Northern Territory, possession of psilocybin mushrooms for personal use remains a criminal offence. Penalties vary by state and quantity, ranging from fines and diversion programmes to potential imprisonment for larger amounts.

In the ACT, possession of up to 0.25g of psilocybin (the active compound) for personal use is a civil matter, not criminal, since October 2023. This applies to personal possession only and does not extend to cultivation or supply.

## The Therapeutic Access Pathway

If you are living with treatment-resistant depression and have not responded to at least two lines of conventional antidepressants, you may be eligible to access psilocybin-assisted therapy through an authorised psychiatrist under the TGA Special Access Scheme Category B. This is a legitimate legal option in 2026, though access remains limited by the number of approved prescribers and the substantial cost of treatment.

This is not microdosing. Therapeutic psilocybin sessions use full doses in a supervised clinical setting over several hours. Microdosing is not part of any currently approved Australian therapeutic protocol. ## What the Legal Risk Actually Looks Like

If you are microdosing in Australia outside the ACT, you are doing so outside the law. The realistic risk of prosecution for personal use quantities is low in most states, but it is not zero. Quantity thresholds matter, and anything that could be interpreted as supply, including sharing with a friend, carries a significantly higher risk.

## Where Australian Law Is Heading

The political momentum is broadly in the direction of further reform. Clinical trial data from Australian institutions continues to build a positive evidence base, and several state governments have signalled interest in harm reduction policy reviews. It is plausible that the next five years will bring expanded therapeutic access frameworks and possibly further decriminalisation in one or more states.

But in 2026, personal use microdosing remains illegal in most of Australia. That is the accurate answer, even if it is not the one most people want to hear.
