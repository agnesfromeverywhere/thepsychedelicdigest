The psychedelic clinical trials landscape has never been more active or more promising. With major studies reading out in depression, PTSD, substance use disorders, and neurodegenerative diseases, 2026 is shaping up to be a pivotal year. Here are the 20+ trials to watch.

## The Trials That Could Change Everything

The last three years have seen an unprecedented surge in psychedelic research. Propelled by [regulatory milestones](https://www.thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/psychedelic-therapy-australia-2026), [major investments](https://www.thepsychedelicdigest.com/psyfinance), and a growing public appetite for new mental health solutions, the field is moving at a pace that would have been unimaginable a decade ago.

As of July 2026, there are over 100 active psychedelic clinical trials worldwide. But a handful stand out for their potential to fundamentally reshape how we treat mental health conditions.

**These are the heavyweights. The game-changers. The studies that could make psychedelic medicine a first-line treatment option for millions of people.**

And they are all reading out in the next 12 months.

## Psilocybin for Treatment-Resistant Depression

Leading the pack is PSILODEP-2, Monash University's phase 2 randomised controlled trial of psilocybin-assisted therapy for treatment-resistant depression.

With 160 participants across multiple sites, it is the largest psilocybin trial in the Southern Hemisphere. And its results could be transformative.

**Why it matters:** Treatment-resistant depression affects 100 million people worldwide. One-third of all depression patients do not respond to existing medications. If psilocybin proves effective for even a fraction of these cases, it would be the biggest breakthrough in depression treatment in 30 years.

**What to watch:** The study completes in November 2026. Expect top-line results in Q1 2027.

## MDMA for PTSD

Next up is MAPS-2, the confirmatory phase 3 trial of MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD.

Building on the landmark results of MAPS' first phase 3 study, which found that 67% of participants no longer qualified for a PTSD diagnosis after three MDMA sessions, MAPS-2 could cement MDMA as the gold standard PTSD treatment.

**Why it matters:** PTSD affects 354 million people globally and is notoriously difficult to treat. Current first-line therapies like SSRIs and prolonged exposure have high dropout rates and often fail to produce remission. If MAPS-2 replicates the phase 3 results, MDMA could become the first FDA-approved medication for PTSD.

**What to watch:** Enrolment is ongoing, with data expected in late 2026 or early 2027.

## LSD for Alcohol Use Disorder

MindMed's phase 2b trial of LSD for alcohol use disorder is the most advanced psychedelic study in addiction.

With 200 participants across seven sites, it is also the largest LSD study in more than 50 years.

**Why it matters:** Alcohol kills 3 million people annually. Existing treatments like naltrexone have limited efficacy and adherence. If LSD proves effective, it could open up a new paradigm in addiction treatment centred on the powerful insights and behaviour changes catalyzed by psychedelic experiences.

**What to watch:** The study is fully enrolled, with results expected in Q4 2026.

## Psilocybin for Alzheimer's Disease

Perhaps the most intriguing study on the horizon is the upcoming Phase 2 trial of psilocybin for Alzheimer's disease, led by Johns Hopkins.

Preclinical data has shown that psychedelics can promote neuroplasticity, reduce inflammation, and even stimulate the growth of new neural connections. But this will be the first rigorous human trial exploring those effects in a neurodegenerative disease.

**Why it matters:** Alzheimer's affects 6 million Americans and has been called the greatest unmet medical need of our time. There has not been a new FDA-approved Alzheimer's drug in nearly 20 years. If psilocybin shows even modest efficacy in slowing cognitive decline, it would be a landmark result.

**What to watch:** The trial is set to begin enrolling in late 2026, with data likely in 2028.
