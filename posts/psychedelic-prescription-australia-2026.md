**TL;DR:** Only psychiatrists who have received specific TGA authorisation can prescribe psilocybin or MDMA in Australia. As of 2026, fewer than 100 hold this status nationally. Here is how the system works and how to navigate it as a patient.

---

## Who Is Legally Allowed to Prescribe Psychedelic Therapy in Australia?

In Australia, only **registered specialist psychiatrists** who have been individually approved by the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) can legally prescribe psilocybin or MDMA as part of a therapeutic programme.

Your GP cannot prescribe it. A psychologist cannot administer it. A therapist cannot prescribe it even if they are trained in the therapy component.

The prescribing role belongs exclusively to an Authorised Prescriber psychiatrist, and the TGA approves each psychiatrist individually for each substance they wish to prescribe.

---

## How Does a Psychiatrist Become an Authorised Prescriber?

The pathway is deliberately rigorous and involves multiple stages.

**Step 1: Specialist registration**
The psychiatrist must be a registered specialist with the Medical Board of Australia and a Fellow of the Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Psychiatrists (RANZCP).

**Step 2: Specialist training in psychedelic therapy**
The psychiatrist must complete recognised training in psychedelic-assisted therapy. Several programs are now available, including the Certificate in Psychedelic-Assisted Therapies and a Graduate Certificate in Psychedelic-Assisted PsychoSomatic Therapy accredited through RANZCP.

**Step 3: Ethics committee approval**
Before applying to the TGA, the psychiatrist must submit a detailed clinical protocol to a Human Research Ethics Committee (HREC) registered with the National Health and Medical Research Council. This outlines their clinical rationale, patient selection criteria, safety protocols, and integration support approach. HREC approval can take three to six months.

**Step 4: TGA Authorised Prescriber application**
With HREC approval in hand, the psychiatrist applies to the TGA for Authorised Prescriber status for each specific substance. The TGA reviews the application and grants individual authorisation.

**Step 5: Importing the substance**
Even after AP status is granted, the psychiatrist or their clinic must obtain an import permit from the Office of Drug Control to access pharmaceutical-grade psilocybin or MDMA. Supply currently comes primarily through Optimi Health in Canada.

This process takes most psychiatrists between six months and two years from initial training to treating their first patient.

## How Many Authorised Prescribers Are There in Australia?

As of mid-2026, **fewer than 100 psychiatrists nationally hold Authorised Prescriber status** for psilocybin, MDMA, or both.

This number is growing, but slowly. The training pipeline, ethics review process, and TGA application timeline mean that scaling prescriber numbers takes years rather than months.

The geographic distribution is heavily skewed toward Sydney and Melbourne, with limited presence in regional Australia, Western Australia, and the Northern Territory.

## Which Conditions Can Be Treated Under the Authorised Prescriber Scheme?

The TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme currently covers two conditions only:

- **Psilocybin** for treatment-resistant depression (TRD), defined as depression that has not responded to at least two adequate antidepressant trials
- **MDMA** for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), with specific clinical eligibility criteria updated in 2026 guidelines

All other conditions, including general anxiety, OCD, addiction, and eating disorders, are not currently covered under the AP scheme and require clinical trial access instead.

## How Do You Get a Referral to an Authorised Prescriber?

This is where the process becomes genuinely difficult for most patients, because of a constraint that surprises many people.

**The TGA prohibits authorised prescribers and clinics from publicly advertising that they offer psilocybin or MDMA therapy.** Schedule 8 medicines cannot be promoted to the public. This means most of the psychiatrists and clinics offering this service cannot say so on their websites.

The result is a system where the service exists but is nearly invisible to the patients who need it most.

**The realistic pathways to finding an Authorised Prescriber:**

- **Ask your current psychiatrist directly** whether they hold AP status or can refer you to someone who does
- **Ask your GP** for a referral to a psychiatrist with an interest in psychedelic-assisted therapy
- **Contact clinics that openly discuss the pathway** without advertising the prescription service directly, such as GoodMind Therapeutics and Inner Focus Therapy
- **Contact university psychiatry departments** in Sydney or Melbourne and ask about AP-connected referral networks

The process requires patience and persistence. It is not as simple as booking an appointment.

## Why Can Clinics Not Advertise This Service?

Under the Therapeutic Goods Act 1989 and the TGA Advertising Code, all Schedule 8 medicines are prohibited from being advertised to the public. This includes psilocybin and MDMA.

The law states explicitly that **it is illegal for prescribers or healthcare facilities to indicate that they can prescribe or supply MDMA or psilocybin**. Penalties are substantial.

This creates a paradox: the treatment is legal, but advertising its availability is not. Patients cannot easily find who offers it, and clinicians cannot easily tell them.

It is one of the most significant access barriers in the current system, and one that patient advocacy groups are actively pushing to reform.

## What Does Treatment Actually Cost?

A full course of psychedelic-assisted therapy through the Authorised Prescriber scheme costs between **$20,000 and $40,000 AUD** out of pocket. Medicare does not cover it. Private health insurance coverage is rare.

The Department of Veterans' Affairs has agreed to fund treatment on a case-by-case basis for eligible veterans with PTSD, which is a meaningful development for that population.

For everyone else, the cost remains the most prohibitive barrier. ## FAQ

**Can my GP refer me for psilocybin therapy in Australia?**
Your GP can write a referral to a psychiatrist, but cannot prescribe psilocybin themselves. The prescribing must come from a TGA Authorised Prescriber psychiatrist.

**Can a psychologist administer psychedelic therapy in Australia?**
Psychologists can provide preparation and integration therapy as part of the treatment team, but cannot prescribe the substance. Only Authorised Prescriber psychiatrists can prescribe.

**Is there a public list of Authorised Prescribers in Australia?**
No. The TGA does not publish a public list of APs. This is one of the most significant navigation barriers in the current system.

**What if my psychiatrist is not an Authorised Prescriber?**
Ask them to refer you to a colleague who is, or ask whether they are interested in pursuing AP status. The training pathways are now well established.

**How long does the treatment process take from referral to first session?**
From GP referral to completing treatment, expect a minimum of three to six months. Finding an AP psychiatrist, completing assessments, and completing the full protocol all take time.
