## Why Volunteer for a Psychedelic Trial?

Legal psilocybin or MDMA therapy in Australia costs between $20,000 and $40,000 AUD out of pocket through the TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme. For most Australians, that number is prohibitive.

Clinical trials offer a legitimate alternative. Participants receive the same pharmaceutical-grade substances, the same clinical oversight, and the same preparation and integration support, at no cost. In some trials, participants are also compensated for their time and travel.

This is the most underutilised access pathway in Australia, and the least talked about outside of specialist circles.

## How Many Trials Are Running in Australia Right Now?

As of mid-2026, there are approximately 25 active psychedelic-related clinical trials in Australia, with more in planning or ethics review stages.

The number has grown significantly since 2023, when TGA rescheduling removed key legal barriers for researchers. Institutions now actively running or recruiting for trials include:

- **University of Melbourne**
- **Monash University**
- **University of Queensland**
- **Macquarie University, Sydney**
- **Australian National University (ANU)**
- **St Vincent's Hospital, Melbourne and Sydney**
- **The Black Dog Institute**

Substances being studied include psilocybin, MDMA, ketamine, and in some cases ayahuasca-derived compounds.

## What Conditions Do Trials Currently Cover?

The current trial landscape in Australia covers a wider range of conditions than the TGA's prescribing scheme, which is limited to treatment-resistant depression and PTSD.

**Conditions currently being studied in Australian trials include:**

- Treatment-resistant depression
- PTSD
- End-of-life anxiety and existential distress
- Alcohol use disorder
- Eating disorders, particularly anorexia nervosa
- Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
- Chronic pain
- General anxiety disorder
- Borderline personality disorder (in planning stages)

If your condition is not on this list, it is still worth checking the current registry, as new trials open regularly.

## What Does the Process Look Like?

Joining a clinical trial is not as simple as signing up for a gym membership. The process is rigorous, and for good reason. Here is what to expect.

**Step 1: Find an eligible trial**
Search clinicaltrials.gov with Australia as the location and your condition as the search term. Note the trial ID and the primary contact institution.

**Step 2: Expression of interest**
Contact the trial team via the listed email or phone. Most trials have a research coordinator who manages initial enquiries. You will typically complete a brief screening questionnaire online or by phone.

**Step 3: Full screening assessment**
If you pass initial screening, you will be invited to a detailed assessment. This usually involves a psychiatric evaluation, a medical history review, and sometimes blood tests or an ECG. This stage can take one to three appointments.

**Step 4: Informed consent**
If you are eligible, you will receive a detailed explanation of the trial protocol, including risks, the possibility of receiving a placebo, and what is expected of you as a participant. You sign a formal consent document.

**Step 5: Treatment**
Trial protocols vary, but most include two to three preparation sessions, one or two supervised dosing sessions in a clinical setting, and post-session integration therapy. The full treatment period typically runs four to eight weeks.

**Step 6: Follow-up**
Most trials include follow-up assessments at one, three, and six months post-treatment. Your data contributes to the evidence base that shapes future policy and access.

## How Do You Find and Apply?

**Primary registry:** [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov)
Search: "psilocybin Australia" or "MDMA Australia" or your specific condition plus Australia.

**Australian New Zealand Clinical Trials Registry:** [anzctr.org.au](https://www.anzctr.org.au)
This registry includes trials not always listed on the US-based clinicaltrials.gov, and is specifically focused on Australia and New Zealand.

**University research portals:** Check the psychology and psychiatry department websites of major Australian universities directly. Many recruit through their own channels before listing publicly.

**Your GP or psychiatrist:** If you are already in the mental health system, ask your treating clinician directly. Many trial referrals come through existing clinical relationships.

## What Are the Trade-offs?

Clinical trials are not perfect. Here is what to weigh before applying.

**You may receive a placebo.** Most trials are randomised and controlled. You may be assigned to a placebo group, especially in phase 2 trials. Many trials offer crossover, meaning placebo participants receive the active treatment in a second phase.

**The protocol is fixed.** Unlike private therapy, you cannot customise the treatment to your needs. The dose, timing, and approach are determined by the study design.

**Eligibility criteria are strict.** Most trials exclude people with a personal or family history of psychosis, certain cardiac conditions, current suicidality, or specific medication combinations. Read the eligibility criteria carefully before investing time in the process.

**It takes longer.** From expression of interest to first dosing session, the process can take two to six months depending on the trial and your eligibility.

The trade-off of free, medically supervised treatment against these limitations is worth considering seriously, particularly for people who have already exhausted standard treatment options.

## FAQ

**How do I know if I am eligible for a psychedelic clinical trial in Australia?**
Eligibility varies by trial. The most common exclusion criteria are personal or family history of psychosis or schizophrenia, active suicidal ideation, certain heart conditions, and specific medication interactions. Check the inclusion and exclusion criteria listed on each trial's clinicaltrials.gov page.

**Do I get paid to participate in a trial?**
Some trials offer reimbursement for travel and time, typically $50 to $150 per visit. You are not paid for participating in treatment itself.

**Can I choose psilocybin or MDMA?**
No. Each trial tests a specific substance for a specific condition. You apply to a trial based on the substance and condition being studied.

**What if there are no trials near me?**
Many Australian trials now offer telehealth for preparation and integration sessions, with only the dosing session requiring in-person attendance. Geographic barriers are reducing.

**Is the treatment in a trial the same quality as private therapy?**
In most cases, yes and often higher. Trial protocols are closely monitored, the therapists are specifically trained, and outcomes are rigorously tracked. The main difference is less personalisation.
