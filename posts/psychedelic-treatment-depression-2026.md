## Is Psilocybin Therapy for Depression Legal in Australia?

Yes. Since July 1, 2023, authorised psychiatrists in Australia can legally prescribe psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression under the TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme.

Australia was the first country in the world to legalise this at a national regulatory level.

The caveat: **legal does not mean accessible**. Fewer than 100 psychiatrists nationally hold Authorised Prescriber status, treatment costs between $20,000 and $40,000 AUD, and Medicare does not cover it. The regulatory milestone and the access reality are very different things.

## What Does the Research Show?

The evidence base for psilocybin in treatment-resistant depression is among the strongest in psychedelic medicine.

**Key studies:**

A landmark 2021 study by researchers at Imperial College London published in the New England Journal of Medicine compared psilocybin-assisted therapy to daily escitalopram (a common antidepressant) over six weeks. **Psilocybin produced significantly greater remission rates**, with 57% of participants in the psilocybin group showing marked improvement compared to 28% in the escitalopram group.

A 2025 meta-analysis covering 11 randomised controlled trials found that psilocybin consistently outperformed placebo for treatment-resistant depression, with effect sizes described as large to very large by the study authors.

The most clinically significant finding across studies is **durability**. While antidepressants require daily dosing, often indefinitely, the benefits of one to two psilocybin sessions have been shown to persist for months to years in multiple studies.

According to Professor Robin Carhart-Harris, a leading psychedelic neuroscientist who has worked at both Imperial College London and UCSF, psilocybin appears to work by increasing the brain's flexibility and reducing the rigid, self-referential thought patterns that characterise chronic depression.

## Who Qualifies for Treatment in Australia?

Under the TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme, psilocybin therapy for depression is restricted to patients with **treatment-resistant depression (TRD)**, defined as:

- A diagnosis of major depressive disorder
- Failure to respond to at least two adequate antidepressant trials at therapeutic doses for sufficient duration
- Assessment by an Authorised Prescriber psychiatrist confirming suitability

**Additional eligibility considerations include:**

- No personal or family history of psychosis or schizophrenia
- No active suicidal ideation with intent or plan
- No current severe substance use disorder
- Cardiovascular health sufficient for the physical demands of the experience
- Willingness to engage in preparation and integration therapy

## What Does a Full Treatment Programme Look Like?

A full course of psilocybin-assisted therapy in Australia involves multiple stages spread across four to eight weeks.

### Preparation Phase (2 to 3 sessions)

Before any psilocybin is administered, you work with a trained therapist, and in some cases the prescribing psychiatrist, to:

- Review your mental health history and current symptoms
- Set clear intentions for the experience
- Discuss what psilocybin does, what to expect, and how to work with difficult material that may arise
- Build rapport with the clinical team who will support you during the dosing session

Preparation is not optional. The quality of this phase significantly affects the outcome of the dosing session.

### Dosing Session (1 to 2 sessions)

The dosing session takes place in a specially prepared clinical room, designed to feel calm and comfortable rather than medical. You are accompanied throughout by two trained therapists or therapist-psychiatrist pairs.

**The typical psilocybin dose used in Australian clinical settings is 25mg**, administered as a capsule. The session lasts approximately six to eight hours. You wear an eye mask for much of it and listen to a curated music playlist. The therapists do not guide or direct the experience. They are present to support, reassure, and hold a safe container.

Most people describe the experience as the most significant of their lives.

### Integration Phase (3 to 6 sessions)

What happens after the dosing session is as important as the session itself. Integration therapy helps you make sense of what emerged, connect the experience to your life, and embed the insights into lasting change.

Without adequate integration support, the clinical benefit of psilocybin therapy is significantly reduced. This is why integration therapy is a non-negotiable part of any credible programme.

## How Long Do the Effects Last?

This is the most compelling part of the clinical picture.

Standard antidepressants require daily dosing, often indefinitely, and lose effectiveness for many patients over time. **Psilocybin therapy involves one to two sessions** and has been shown to produce sustained antidepressant effects lasting months to years.

A 2022 follow-up study from Imperial College London found that **67% of participants who responded to psilocybin therapy were still in remission at six months** with no additional treatment. A five-year follow-up study from Johns Hopkins, published in 2024, found sustained improvements in the majority of participants from a single psilocybin session.

The durability of the effect is not fully understood mechanistically, but leading researchers believe it relates to the neuroplasticity window that psilocybin opens, during which the brain is more capable of forming new patterns.

## What Does It Cost and Who Can Access It?

A full programme through the TGA Authorised Prescriber scheme costs between **$20,000 and $40,000 AUD** out of pocket. Medicare does not cover it. Private health insurance coverage is rare and inconsistent.

**Who currently has practical access:**

- People who can afford the full out-of-pocket cost
- Eligible veterans, whose treatment may be funded by the Department of Veterans' Affairs on a case-by-case basis
- Clinical trial participants who receive the treatment free as part of a research protocol

**Who does not currently have access:**

- Most Australians without significant financial resources
- People in regional and rural areas with limited specialist access
- People with conditions not covered by the AP scheme

## FAQ

**How effective is psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression?**
Multiple clinical trials show response rates of 50 to 70 percent in people who have not responded to standard antidepressants. Effect sizes are large by clinical standards, and effects persist for months to years after a single course of treatment.

**Does psilocybin therapy work if antidepressants have failed?**
Yes. This is specifically why it is approved for treatment-resistant depression. The mechanism of action is different from SSRIs, which is part of why it works for people who have not responded to standard medication.

**How many psilocybin sessions are needed for depression?**
Most Australian clinical protocols involve one to two supervised dosing sessions, supported by two to three preparation sessions and three to six integration sessions.

**Is psilocybin addictive?**
No. Psilocybin does not produce physical dependence or addiction. It also does not produce the kind of tolerance that makes repeated use reinforcing in the way addictive substances do.

**Can I microdose instead of doing full therapy for depression?**
Microdosing is not a clinical treatment for depression and should not be used to replace prescribed medication without medical guidance.
