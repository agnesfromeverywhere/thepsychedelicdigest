## Psychedelic Therapy Is Now Legal in Australia

Psychedelic therapy is legal in Australia. Since 1 July 2023, authorised psychiatrists can prescribe psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression and MDMA for PTSD under strict clinical controls. Australia was the first country in the world to establish this national regulated pathway.

Most people have heard the headline. Almost nobody knows what to actually do with that information.

If you have treatment-resistant depression or PTSD and you are wondering whether this applies to you, the answer is: possibly yes. But the path from curiosity to a clinical session is not obvious. The information is scattered across government websites, buried in academic papers, or wrapped in jargon that assumes you already know what an "Authorised Prescriber Scheme" is.

This guide fixes that. No jargon. No hype. Just what you need to know.

## What Is Psychedelic Therapy?

Psychedelic therapy is a structured clinical treatment that combines a psychedelic substance with professional psychological support. It is not the same as taking a substance and hoping for the best.

Researchers and clinicians describe three essential phases:

Preparation: A series of talk-therapy sessions with a trained therapist before any medicine is taken. The goal is to build therapeutic trust, explore the patient's history, and set a clear intention for the experience ahead.

The dosing session: The patient takes the prescribed medicine in a clinical setting, lying down, typically with an eye mask and music. One or two trained therapists are present throughout. Each session lasts six or more hours.

Integration: A series of therapy sessions after the dosing experience to make sense of what surfaced, translate insights into lasting change, and process anything difficult or confusing. This phase can continue for weeks or months.

Without all three phases, the treatment is incomplete. The medicine is the key. The therapy is the door.

## Is Psychedelic Therapy Legal in Australia?

Yes. As of 1 July 2023, psychedelic therapy is legal in Australia for two specific conditions.

The TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration) rescheduled psilocybin and MDMA from Schedule 9 (prohibited substances) to Schedule 8 (controlled medicines) for specific clinical uses. Outside those approved uses, both substances remain prohibited.

Australia is the first country in the world to establish a national, regulated clinical model for these therapies.

## Who Qualifies for Psychedelic Therapy in Australia?

Currently, two conditions are approved:

Psilocybin is approved for treatment-resistant depression (TRD), defined as depression that has not responded to at least two conventional treatments such as antidepressants or psychotherapy.

MDMA is approved for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).

Some people with co-occurring mental health conditions may also be eligible. However, people with a personal or family history of bipolar disorder, psychosis, or certain other psychiatric conditions are not considered suitable under current safety protocols.

If you do not qualify today, the framework is designed to evolve as research expands. Clinical trials into psilocybin for anxiety, addiction, and OCD are active in Australia right now.

## How Do You Access Psychedelic Therapy in Australia?

### Step 1: Start with your GP

Your general practitioner cannot prescribe psilocybin or MDMA, but they are the first step. Explain your diagnosis, your treatment history, and what has not worked. Ask for a referral to a psychiatrist with experience in psychedelic-assisted therapy.

### Step 2: Find an Authorised Psychiatrist

Not every psychiatrist can prescribe these medicines. Only psychiatrists registered with AHPRA with specialist psychiatry registration and a completed Fellowship with the RANZCP can apply for Authorised Prescriber status.

### Step 3: Clinical Assessment

Once connected with an authorised psychiatrist, you will undergo a thorough assessment to determine whether you are a suitable candidate.

### Step 4: Preparation Sessions

If approved, you will complete a series of talk-therapy sessions before any medicine is involved.

### Step 5: The Dosing Session

You take the prescribed medicine in a clinical setting. You lie down, typically with an eye mask on and music playing. One or two therapists sit with you throughout.

### Step 6: Integration

Integration therapy begins immediately after and continues across multiple sessions. This is where the clinical value is consolidated.

## How Much Does Psychedelic Therapy Cost in Australia?

Psychedelic therapy is expensive.

Average prices in 2026 sit at:

- $10,000 AUD for a single dosing session including preparation and integration
- $30,000 AUD for a full three-dose MDMA-assisted therapy program

That is beyond the reach of most Australians.

Medibank committed $10 million AUD to fund MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD for eligible members, in partnership with Emyria.

The Department of Veterans' Affairs also committed funding for eligible veterans with PTSD.

## What About Microdosing?

Microdosing is not covered by the 2023 TGA decision.

Outside the clinical prescribing pathway, psilocybin remains a Schedule 9 prohibited substance in Australia.

## Is Psychedelic Therapy Safe?

Within a clinical setting, with proper preparation and integration, psychedelic therapy carries a meaningfully different risk profile from recreational use.

The clinical container is not optional. This is not a medicine you take at home. It is a medicine you take inside a carefully constructed therapeutic relationship.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is psilocybin legal in Australia?

Psilocybin is legal only when prescribed by a TGA-authorised psychiatrist for treatment-resistant depression.

### Can my GP prescribe psychedelic therapy?

No. Only authorised psychiatrists can prescribe psilocybin or MDMA for therapy.

### Does Medicare cover psychedelic therapy in Australia?

Not currently. Some private insurers have begun covering it for eligible members.
