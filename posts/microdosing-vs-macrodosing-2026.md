Microdosing and macrodosing are not points on the same dial. They are fundamentally different approaches to psychedelics with different mechanisms, different goals, different risks, and different outcomes. Microdosing means taking a sub-perceptual dose, typically one-tenth to one-twentieth of a full dose, on a structured schedule with no hallucinogenic effect. Macrodosing means taking a full or high dose with the intention of a complete psychedelic experience, including significant alterations in perception, emotion, and consciousness.

Here is how to understand the difference and decide which, if either, is appropriate for you.

## What Is Microdosing?

A microdose is sub-perceptual. You should not hallucinate. You should not lose your sense of self. You should not feel significantly impaired. The goal is a subtle shift, often described as enhanced mood, improved focus, or greater emotional openness, that fits inside a normal day. Most people using a structured protocol like the Fadiman Protocol dose in the morning and go about their work, parenting, or creative practice without disruption.

## What Is Macrodosing?

A macrodose, sometimes called a full dose or a ceremonial dose, is a quantity large enough to produce a full psychedelic experience. For psilocybin mushrooms, this typically begins at 2g of dried mushrooms for a mild experience and can range up to 5g or more for a very intense one. For LSD, a macrodose is generally 100 micrograms and above.

A macrodose requires several hours of unstructured time, ideally set and setting that are intentionally prepared, and ideally an experienced guide or trusted companion. It is not compatible with working, driving, or any functional responsibility.

## 5 Key Differences

The first difference is perceptual impact. A microdose produces no hallucinations and minimal perceptual change. A macrodose produces significant alterations in perception, including visual distortion, altered sense of time, and in high doses, complete dissolution of the ordinary sense of self.

The second difference is time commitment. A microdose fits inside a working day. A macrodose typically requires six to eight hours plus recovery time, and should ideally be followed by one or two integration days.

The third difference is the mechanism of therapeutic benefit. Microdosing is thought to work through subtle neuroplasticity and serotonin modulation over time. Macrodosing is thought to work through acute disruption of default mode network activity, producing insight, emotional release, and a reset in entrenched psychological patterns. The research base for macrodosing is considerably more developed. Johns Hopkins, NYU, and Imperial College London have all published positive results for psilocybin-assisted therapy for depression, addiction, and end-of-life anxiety.

The fourth difference is risk profile. Microdosing carries a relatively low acute risk profile, though legal risks vary by jurisdiction. Macrodosing carries higher short-term psychological risk, including the possibility of a difficult or destabilising experience, particularly in people with underlying vulnerability to psychosis or a family history of schizophrenia. The legal risks are broadly the same.

The fifth difference is intent. Most people who microdose are looking for gradual, sustainable improvement in daily functioning. Most people who macrodose are looking for a significant psychological shift, healing, or spiritual experience. These are different tools for different jobs.

## Which Is Right for You?

If you are looking for something subtle, manageable, and compatible with your daily responsibilities, microdosing is the more accessible starting point. It allows you to build familiarity with psychedelics at low risk and develop your own baseline understanding of how your nervous system responds.

If you are dealing with deep-rooted trauma, addiction, or treatment-resistant depression, the clinical evidence increasingly points to macrodosing in a supported therapeutic context as the more powerful intervention. Several articles on this site cover the research in more depth: [thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog).

If you have no experience with psychedelics at all, starting with microdosing is generally the more prudent choice. It allows you to understand your individual sensitivity before engaging with a full-dose experience.

## Can You Do Both?

Yes, though not simultaneously. Many experienced practitioners use macrodosing for major psychological work and microdosing as a maintenance or integration tool in between. These approaches can complement each other, but they should be separated by enough time to allow full integration. Running a microdosing protocol in the weeks immediately following a macrodose is generally not recommended, as the nervous system benefits from a complete rest period.

## What Both Have in Common

Both approaches work best with intention. Approaching either a microdose or a macrodose as a passive intervention, something that happens to you rather than something you actively engage with, consistently produces weaker results in community observation and in the limited research available. Journalling, integration practices, and reflection are valuable regardless of which path you choose.
