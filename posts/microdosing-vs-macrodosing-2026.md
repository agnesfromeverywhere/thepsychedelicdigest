Microdosing and macrodosing are not points on the same dial. They are fundamentally different approaches to psychedelics, with different mechanisms, different goals, different risks, and different outcomes. If you are trying to decide which is right for you, the most important thing to understand is that they are tools built for different jobs.

Here is how to tell them apart, and how to choose.

## What Is Microdosing?

A microdose is sub-perceptual. You should not hallucinate. You should not lose your sense of self or your capacity to function. The goal is a subtle, manageable shift that fits inside a normal day. Most people using the [Fadiman Protocol](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog/the-fadiman-protocol-explained-2026) dose in the morning and go to work, parent their children, or practice their creative craft without any disruption.

For psilocybin mushrooms, a microdose is typically 0.1g to 0.3g of dried material. For LSD, 5 to 15 micrograms. For a full glossary of terms, visit the [Psychedelic Digest glossary](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/glossary).

## What Is Macrodosing?

A macrodose is a full psychedelic experience. For dried psilocybin mushrooms, this begins at around 2g for a mild journey and can reach 5g or more for an intense one. For LSD, 100 micrograms and above.

A macrodose requires hours of unstructured, protected time with no responsibilities. It is not compatible with work, driving, or caring for others. Ideally it involves intentional set and setting, and either an experienced guide or a deeply trusted companion. Clinical research programmes at institutions including [Johns Hopkins Center for Psychedelic and Consciousness Research](https://hopkinspsychedelic.org) and [Imperial College London](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/brain-sciences/research/psychedelics/) use supervised macrodose sessions as the primary therapeutic intervention.

## 5 Key Differences

The first difference is perceptual impact. A microdose produces no hallucinations and minimal perceptual change. A macrodose produces significant alterations in perception, time sense, emotional depth, and at high doses, a complete dissolution of the ordinary sense of self.

The second difference is time commitment. A microdose fits inside a working day. A macrodose typically requires six to eight hours plus one or two integration days afterwards.

The third difference is mechanism. Microdosing is thought to work through gradual neuroplasticity and cumulative serotonin 2A receptor stimulation over time. Macrodosing works through acute disruption of default mode network activity, producing deep insight, emotional processing, and psychological reset. A landmark 2023 study in [Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/nm/) found that a single therapeutic psilocybin dose produced measurable changes in brain connectivity lasting several weeks.

The fourth difference is risk profile. Microdosing carries a relatively low acute psychological risk. Macrodosing carries meaningful short-term risk, including the possibility of a difficult or destabilising experience, particularly for people with a personal or family history of psychosis or schizophrenia.

The fifth difference is intent. People who microdose are typically looking for gradual, sustainable improvements in daily functioning. People who macrodose are typically seeking significant psychological healing, emotional release, or spiritual insight.

## Which Is Right for You?

If you want something subtle and compatible with your daily life, microdosing is the more accessible entry point. It lets you build familiarity with how your nervous system responds to psychedelics before engaging with anything more intense.

If you are dealing with treatment-resistant depression, deep trauma, addiction, or end-of-life anxiety, the clinical evidence increasingly supports macrodosing in a therapeutic context as the more powerful intervention. Several guides on this site explore the research in depth: [thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog](https://thepsychedelicdigest.com/blog).

If you have no prior experience with psychedelics, starting with microdosing is almost always the wiser first step.

## Can You Do Both?

Yes, though not simultaneously. Many experienced practitioners use macrodosing for major psychological work and microdosing as an integration and maintenance tool between sessions. These approaches complement each other, but they should be well separated in time. Running a microdosing protocol immediately after a macrodose is not recommended. The nervous system needs a full rest period before re-engaging with any psychedelic practice.

## What Both Have in Common

Both approaches work best with intention. Passive consumption, treating either a microdose or a macrodose as something that simply happens to you, consistently produces weaker outcomes than engaged, intentional practice. Journalling, integration, and reflection are valuable regardless of which path you choose.
