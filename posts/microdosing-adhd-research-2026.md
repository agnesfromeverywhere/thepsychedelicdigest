## Why Are People With ADHD Microdosing?

ADHD affects an estimated **one in twenty Australians**, and standard treatments, primarily stimulant medications like dexamphetamine and methylphenidate, do not work for everyone. Side effects including appetite suppression, sleep disruption, and cardiovascular impact lead many people to look for alternatives.

Microdosing psilocybin has emerged as one of the most discussed alternatives in ADHD communities globally. Online forums, Reddit threads, and community surveys consistently show that people with ADHD report subjective improvements in focus, emotional regulation, and impulsivity when microdosing.

But self-report is not clinical evidence. Here is what the actual research shows.

---

## What Does the Research Show?

The research base for microdosing and ADHD specifically is thin but growing.

A 2022 study published in the Journal of Psychoactive Drugs tracked adults with ADHD who chose to microdose on their own initiative. **Over four weeks, participants reported significant reductions in inattention and hyperactivity**, alongside improvements in emotional regulation. The effect sizes were comparable to stimulant medications in some domains.

The critical limitation: no placebo control. Every participant knew they were taking a psychedelic. Expectation effect cannot be ruled out.

A larger naturalistic study from Imperial College London published in eLife tracked over 650 microdosers across conditions. **People who identified as having ADHD showed the largest subjective improvements of any subgroup**, including greater reductions in depression, anxiety, and distractibility than neurotypical participants.

According to Dr Vince Polito, a researcher at Macquarie University who has studied microdosing longitudinally in Australia, people with ADHD may respond more strongly to microdosing because of how psilocybin interacts with dopamine and serotonin systems that are already dysregulated in ADHD brains.

Proper randomised controlled trials are now underway at several institutions. Results are expected between 2026 and 2028.

## How Does Psilocybin Affect the ADHD Brain?

ADHD is primarily associated with dysregulation in the dopamine and norepinephrine systems, which affect attention, working memory, and impulse control. Standard medications work by increasing dopamine availability in the prefrontal cortex.

Psilocybin works differently. It primarily targets serotonin receptors, particularly 5-HT2A, but its downstream effects include:

- **Increased default mode network flexibility**, reducing the mental rigidity that contributes to task-switching difficulty in ADHD
- **Improved prefrontal cortex connectivity**, which supports executive function and impulse control
- **Increased neuroplasticity**, potentially helping the brain form new, more organised attention pathways
- **Reduction in rumination**, which is a significant secondary symptom for many people with ADHD

The mechanism is different from stimulants, which is exactly why some people who do not respond to medication find psilocybin more effective.

## What Do People With ADHD Actually Report?

Community surveys and naturalistic studies consistently show the same pattern of self-reported effects in people with ADHD who microdose:

**Most commonly reported improvements:**
- Easier task initiation and follow-through
- Reduced emotional dysregulation and reactivity
- Less mental hyperactivity and internal noise
- Improved working memory on dosing days
- Better sleep quality on non-dosing days
- Reduced reliance on caffeine and other stimulants

**Most commonly reported difficulties:**
- Anxiety on dosing days, particularly at higher microdoses
- Inconsistency, with some days working well and others not
- Difficulty finding the right dose without professional guidance
- Legal risk and sourcing uncertainty in Australia

## What Are the Risks Specific to ADHD?

People with ADHD face some specific considerations when microdosing that are worth understanding clearly.

**Stimulant medication interactions:** The combination of psilocybin with dexamphetamine or methylphenidate is not well studied. Some people report that stimulants blunt the effects of psilocybin. Others report increased anxiety. Medical guidance is essential if you are on prescribed ADHD medication.

**Impulsivity risk:** ADHD is associated with impulsivity. Starting a microdosing protocol without structure, clear protocols, and ideally professional support increases the risk of irregular dosing, dose escalation, and unplanned use.

**Emotional amplification:** Psilocybin can surface difficult emotions. For people with ADHD who already struggle with emotional regulation, this can be destabilising without adequate support.

## What Legal Options Exist in Australia for ADHD and Psychedelics?

Currently, none directly. Psilocybin is only legally prescribable in Australia for treatment-resistant depression and PTSD. ADHD is not an approved indication under the TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme.

This means Australians with ADHD who want to explore psilocybin legally have limited options:

- **Clinical trials:** Some trials include ADHD as a secondary measure or comorbidity. Check [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov) for active Australian trials.
- **Compassionate access:** In rare cases, psychiatrists can apply for special access for conditions outside approved indications. This is uncommon and not guaranteed.
- **Ketamine therapy:** Legal and available in Australia, ketamine affects overlapping brain systems and some psychiatrists are exploring its use for ADHD. Costs range from $500 to $1,500 AUD per session.

## FAQ

**Can microdosing replace ADHD medication?**
Not based on current evidence. Microdosing is not a clinically approved treatment for ADHD and should not be used to replace prescribed medication without medical guidance.

**What is the best microdosing protocol for ADHD?**
Most people with ADHD report better results with the Fadiman Protocol, one day on and two days off, rather than daily dosing. Daily dosing tends to build tolerance quickly and can increase anxiety.

**Is microdosing psilocybin legal in Australia for ADHD?**
No. Psilocybin is only legally prescribable for treatment-resistant depression and PTSD under the TGA's Authorised Prescriber scheme. ADHD is not a current approved indication.

**How long does it take to notice effects for ADHD?**
Most people report noticing something within the first one to three dosing days. Consistent, stable effects typically emerge after two to four weeks of regular protocol use.

**Are there Australian researchers studying microdosing and ADHD?**
Yes. Dr Vince Polito at Macquarie University has been at the forefront of naturalistic microdosing research in Australia. Several universities are now running or planning trials that include ADHD populations.
