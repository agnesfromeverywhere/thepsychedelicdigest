Psychedelic medicine had a very good week.

On June 22, 2026, Definium Therapeutics (NASDAQ: DFTX), formerly known as MindMed, announced positive topline results from its Phase 3 Emerge trial. The drug is DT120, an orally disintegrating LSD tablet for major depressive disorder. It met its primary endpoint. It met every key secondary endpoint. It showed no serious adverse events and no suicidality signal.

Then the company raised $700 million. In 24 hours.

If you have been watching this sector and wondering when the "moment" would arrive, this is probably it.

## What Is Definium Therapeutics and What Is DT120?

Definium Therapeutics is a late-stage clinical biopharmaceutical company headquartered in New York, listed on Nasdaq under the ticker DFTX. You may know it by its former name: MindMed, one of the earliest publicly listed psychedelic medicine companies.

The rebrand to Definium was deliberate. The company wants to be seen as serious pharmaceutical infrastructure, not psychedelic hype. Given what just happened in the clinic, that positioning is holding up.

DT120 is an LSD-based compound delivered as an orally disintegrating tablet (ODT), which means it dissolves under the tongue at a precise 100-microgram dose. It is designed for major depressive disorder (MDD), one of the most prevalent and treatment-resistant conditions in global psychiatry. Over 280 million people worldwide live with depression. A significant portion do not respond adequately to existing treatments.

DT120 is engineered to address a specific problem that has plagued psychedelic medicine trials: functional unblinding. This is the phenomenon where participants guess whether they received the active drug, which can inflate results and reduce scientific credibility. Definium built its trial design, and its tablet format, specifically to account for this. The FDA was consulted throughout.

## What Did the Phase 3 Emerge Trial Actually Show?

The Emerge study was a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase 3 trial. It enrolled adults with MDD and evaluated a single 100-microgram dose of DT120 ODT versus placebo.

The results:

**Primary endpoint:** 8.1-point placebo-adjusted reduction in MADRS (Montgomery-Åsberg Depression Rating Scale) score at Week 6, with a p-value of less than 0.0001.

**Secondary endpoint:** 7.3-point placebo-adjusted reduction at Week 12, also at p less than 0.0001.

The MADRS runs from 0 to 60. An 8.1-point placebo-adjusted change from a single dose is clinically meaningful. The p-value essentially means the probability that this result is statistical noise is vanishingly small.

Tolerability was strong. 99% of treatment-emergent adverse events were mild to moderate. No serious adverse events. No increase in suicidality.

For a field that has sometimes struggled to produce clean data from complex trial conditions, this is a landmark result.

## Why Did the Stock Jump 49% and Then Raise $700 Million?

Markets responded immediately. DFTX surged nearly 50% on the day of the announcement, trading above $40 per share in premarket after closing at $24.48 on June 18.

Definium then moved fast. The company announced a public offering that was initially proposed at $500 million. Demand was strong enough that it was upsized to $700 million, priced at $34 per share, with 20.6 million shares sold. The offering closed on June 25, 2026.

The bookrunners were J.P. Morgan, Jefferies, Leerink Partners, and BofA Securities, with Evercore ISI and Stifel also acting as bookrunners. These are not fringe players. This is mainstream institutional capital making a directional bet on psychedelic-derived medicine.

The proceeds are earmarked for research and development, commercialisation preparation for DT120 ODT if approved, and working capital. The Ascend trial, the second Phase 3 study for MDD, is ongoing.

For context on the full trajectory: DFTX posted a 444% return over the twelve months leading to this result. Year to date, the stock is up over 170%.

## Is This the Most Important Psychedelic Finance Story of 2026?

Probably yes, at least so far.

Here is the competitive picture. COMPASS Pathways is advancing COMP360, its synthetic psilocybin compound, through Phase 3 for treatment-resistant depression, with additional data expected in the second half of 2026. MAPS completed Phase 3 MDMA data for PTSD. Australia legalised psychedelic-assisted therapy for MDD and PTSD in 2023, the first country to do so.

What makes DFTX distinct is the oral tablet format and the scalability argument. A dissolving LSD tablet taken in a clinical setting is a different commercial proposition from multi-hour supervised psilocybin sessions. It does not eliminate the need for clinical support, but the delivery model is potentially more adaptable to healthcare systems.

That scalability argument is what the $700 million raise is actually funding.

## What Are the Risks Investors Should Know?

Phase 3 success does not mean approved or on pharmacy shelves. The path from here includes FDA regulatory review, which involves its own timeline and uncertainty. The Ascend trial data, the second MDD study, will be critical for the submission package. Reimbursement negotiations with payers will shape who can actually access DT120. The training infrastructure for clinicians is still being built.

The stock has already priced in significant optimism. Anyone considering DFTX as an investment should weigh the commercial execution risks against the clinical milestone already achieved.

## What Does This Mean for Psychedelic Medicine Broadly?

The question the industry has been waiting to answer is whether psychedelic-derived compounds could survive the rigour of Phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials. DT120 has now answered yes.

That matters beyond DFTX. It signals to regulators, payers, and clinicians that this class of treatments can be studied, validated, and potentially delivered within the existing healthcare infrastructure. It legitimises the broader pipeline. It accelerates capital into other programs.

Psychedelic medicine is not fringe anymore. The science is speaking and the money is listening.
